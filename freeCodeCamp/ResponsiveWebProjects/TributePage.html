My Teaching Career

Teaching was always my dream. Well, once I realized that enjoying a good argument isn't enought of a reason to become a lawyer. I've been teaching since 2003 and never saw myself retiring. COVID has changed that.

After working with prepackaged, asyncronous curriculum for K-2, I realized that I want the ability to build it myself. I see a need for digital K-2 curriclum built by someone with more of an education background than a developer background. I'm capable of learning to code and design in an electronic environment, so here I am.

I don't want to completely leave education, but I do want to move into curriclum design and development.

Education Week has an article on how COVID-19 is not necessarily causing teachers to quit. If you read carefully, many teachers feel as though their options are limited. Picking up some new skills can change that.
